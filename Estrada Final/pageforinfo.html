ABOUT HEALTH TECHOLOGY

Techology in healthcare has changed the way we treat patients. We know many things now that we couldnt know without the technology.

In addition to all the technology we have now, with the addition to 5g doctors are going to be able to do lot of things from home. Being able to preform suguries and treat patients from all parts of tghe world.

“In 10 years the electronic medical record will be the minor player, in terms of where a person’s health history lives. Most of that information will be kept on the phone or in a secure cloud, and the patient will be highly engaged with collecting, curating and sharing that data. Most doctor visits will be like calling up a YouTube meets virtual human docs and there will also be an aspect of virtual reality.”

- Leslie Saxon on the Future of Healthcare

Internet has become a main source of medical information

According to the Pew Internet and American Life Project, in a 2004 survey of 8 million seniors who use the internet, only 66% said they searched healthcare information online.


Healthcare facilities are reaching patients using social media

Healthcare facilities, particularly hospitals, are using social media to establish contact with patients, answer questions about practices, launch public awareness campaigns, and perform community outreach.


Better treatment and less suffering

This is by providing new machines, medicines, and treatments that save lives and improve the chance of recovery for billions


Improved patient care and worker efficiency

Nurses and doctors use hand-held computers to record a patent's medical history and check that they are administering the correct treatment.


Doctors are easier to reach and better at their jobs

With the touch of a smartphone doctors can access thousands of pages of medical textbooks.

Ease of Workflow

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics – demand for this sector is expected to increase by 13% from 2016 to 2026.


Lower Healthcare Costs

According to a study from the University of Michigan, shifting from paper to electronic health records reduced the cost of outpatient care by 3%.


Improved Access to Big Data

The Cloud is an invaluable tool for medical research and sharing medical information. Back in 2014 it was the main way to exchange health info.


Better and Safer Data Storage

Cloud computer technology allows for masses of information to be stored at an unbelievably low cost, all without the limitations


Improved Public Health

EHRs provide invaluable data to clinical researchers, helping to advance medical knowledge and the development of treatments for common health problems
